Let \(M_{m,n} (K)\) be the set of all \(m \times n\) matrices with entries in \(K\text{.}\) A function
\begin{equation*} f : M_{m,n} (K) \to K \end{equation*}
is called:
row linear
if, keeping all rows except the \(i\)-th row constant, \(f\) is linear on the \(i\)-th row (for any \(i\)). In other words if
\begin{equation*} f(\mb{r}_1 , \ldots, a \mb{r}_i + b \mb{r}_i^\prime, \ldots ,\mb{r}_m) = a f(\mb{r}_1 , \ldots, \mb{r}_i , \ldots, \mb{r}_m) + b f(\mb{r}_1 , \ldots, \mb{r}_i^\prime, \ldots, \mb{r}_m) \end{equation*}
alternating
if \(f\) reverses sign whenever two rows are switched. In other words
\begin{equation*} f(\mb{r}_1 , \ldots,\mb{r}_i, \ldots , \mb{r}_j, \ldots , \mb{r}_m) = - f(\mb{r}_1 , \ldots,\mb{r}_j, \ldots , \mb{r}_i, \ldots ,\mb{r}_m). \end{equation*}
