Suppose \(A\) is diagonalizable and \(P\) is a change of basis matrix so that
\begin{equation*} P^{-1} A P = \text{Diag} ( \lambda_1, \ldots, \lambda_n ) \end{equation*}
and let
\begin{equation*} \threevec{g_1 (t)}{\vdots}{g_n (t)} = \mb{g} = P^{-1} \mb{f}. \end{equation*}
If
\begin{equation*} y_j (t) = e^{\lambda_j t} \int e^{-\lambda_j t} g_j (t) \diff{t} \end{equation*}
and
\begin{equation*} \mb{y} (t) = \threevec{y_1 (t)}{\vdots}{y_n (t)} \end{equation*}
then
\begin{equation*} \mb{x} (t) = P \,\mb{y} (t) \end{equation*}
is a solution to equation (5.4.1).
