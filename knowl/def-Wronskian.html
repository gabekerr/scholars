Definition 5.5.6.

Given scalar functions \(x_1 (t), x_2(t), \ldots, x_n(t)\text{,}\) their Wronskian is
\begin{equation*} W_{x_1 x_2 \cdots x_n} = \det \left( \left[ \begin{matrix} x_1 (t) \amp x_2(t)\amp \cdots \amp x_n(t) \\ x^\prime_1 (t) \amp x^\prime_2(t)\amp \cdots \amp x^\prime_n(t) \\ \vdots \amp \vdots \amp \amp \vdots\\ x^{(n - 1)}_1 (t) \amp x^{(n - 1)}_2(t)\amp \cdots \amp x^{(n - 1)}_n (t) \end{matrix} \right] \right) \end{equation*}
