An inner product space \(V\) over \(K\) is a vector space along with a pairing
\begin{equation*} \left\langle , \right\rangle : V \times V \to K \end{equation*}
which satisfies
Linear
\begin{equation*} \left\langle \lambda \mb{v}_1 + \mb{v}_2 , \mb{v} \right\rangle = \lambda \left\langle \mb{v}_1 , \mb{v} \right\rangle + \left\langle \mb{v}_2 , \mb{v} \right\rangle. \end{equation*}
Conjugate Symmetric
\begin{equation*} \left\langle \mb{u} , \mb{v} \right\rangle = \overline{\left\langle \mb{v} , \mb{u} \right\rangle}. \end{equation*}
Positive Definite
\begin{equation*} \left\langle \mb{v} , \mb{v} \right\rangle \geq 0 \end{equation*}
with equality if and only if \(\mb{v} = \mb{0}\text{.}\)
