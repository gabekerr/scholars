If \(\mb{r}_1 (t)\text{,}\) \(\mb{r}_2 (t)\) are paths in the inner product space \(V\) over \(K\text{,}\) \(W\) a vector space over \(K\text{,}\) \(T : V \to W\) a linear transformation, and \(\lambda\) is a scalar, then
  1. \((\mb{r}_1 + \lambda \mb{r}_2 ) (t) = \mb{r}_1 (t) + \lambda \mb{r}_2 (t)\) defines the sum of two paths,
  2. \(\left\langle \mb{r}_1 , \mb{r_2} \right\rangle (t) = \left\langle \mb{r}_1 (t) , \mb{r}_2 (t) \right\rangle\) defines the pairing of two paths and gives a scalar function of \(t\text{,}\)
  3. \(T ( \mb{r}_1 ) (t) = T ( \mb{r}_1 (t) )\) is the path of the composition of \(T\) with \(\mb{r}_1\text{.}\)
