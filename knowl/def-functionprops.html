Definition 1.2.8.

Let \(f : A \to B \) be a function.
  1. We say that \(f \) is one-to-one (or 1-1) if the equality \(f(a_0) = f(a_1) \) implies that \(a_0 = a_1 \text{.}\)
  2. We say that \(f \) is onto if \(B \) is the range of \(f \text{.}\)
  3. We say that \(f \) is a one-to-one correspondence if it is both one-to-one and onto.
The element \(b \) from the pair \((a, b) \) is written \(f(a) \) and we think of \(f \) as assigning \(a \) to \(b \text{.}\)
