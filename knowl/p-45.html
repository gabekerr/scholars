The notation for this setup is
\begin{equation} f: A \longrightarrow B \tag{1.2.1} \end{equation}
and the vocabulary around this is
  1. \(gr_f \) is called the graph of \(f \text{,}\)
  2. \(A \) is called the domain of \(f \text{,}\)
  3. \(B \) is called the codomain of \(f \text{,}\)
  4. the set of elements \(b \) in \(B \) that can be written in the form \(f(a) \) for some \(a \) in \(A \) is called the range or image of \(f \text{.}\)
