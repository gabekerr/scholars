Exercise 2.

The unit circle has a `unit hyperbola' analog given by the implicit equation
\begin{equation*} x^2 - y^2 = 1. \end{equation*}
Consider the positive branch of this hyperbola (meaning solutions \((x,y)\) where \(x > 0\text{.}\) There are two very important functions that are sometimes overlooked in a calculus class called the hyperbolic sin and cosine. They are given by the formulas
\begin{align*} \sinh t \amp = \frac{1}{2} \left( e^t - e^{-t} \right), \\ \cosh t \amp = \frac{1}{2} \left( e^t + e^{-t} \right). \end{align*}
(a)
Show that
\begin{equation*} f(t) = \left( \cosh t , \sinh t \right) \end{equation*}
parametrizes the positive branch of the unit hyperbola.
Hint.
(b)
Using complex numbers, show that the ordinary trigonometric functions are related to their hyperbolic cousins by the formulas
\begin{align*} \sin t \amp = - i \sinh (i t), \\ \cos t \amp = \cosh (i t) . \end{align*}
