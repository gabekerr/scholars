Proposition 4.3.4.

Let \(V\) and \(W\) be finite dimensional inner product spaces over \(K\text{.}\) Given a linear transformation
\begin{equation*} T : V \to W \end{equation*}
there is a unique adjoint
\begin{equation*} T^* : W \to V. \end{equation*}

Proof.

Choose orthonormal bases \(\mathcal{B} = \{\mb{v}_1, \ldots, \mb{v}_n \}\) and \(\mathcal{C} = \{\mb{w}_1, \ldots, \mb{w}_m \}\) for \(V\) and \(W\) respectively. Suppose \(A = \cob{T}{\mathcal{B}}{\mathcal{C}}\) represents \(T\) with respect to these bases. ExerciseÂ 4.3.3.1 then shows that the conjugate transpose \(A^*\) represents \(T^*\text{.}\)
