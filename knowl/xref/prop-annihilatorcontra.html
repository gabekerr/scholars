Proposition 2.5.14.

Suppose \(U_1 \leq U_2\) are two vector subspaces of an \(n\)-dimensional space \(V\text{.}\) Then
  1. \(\displaystyle \dim (U_1^\circ) = n - \dim (U_1)\)
  2. \(\displaystyle U_1^{\circ \circ} = U_1\)
  3. \(U_2^\circ \leq U_1^\circ\text{.}\)

Proof.

For the first two statements, let \(\mathcal{B} = \{\mb{u}_1, \ldots, \mb{u}_m, \mb{v}_{m + 1}, \ldots, \mb{v}_n\}\) be a basis for \(V\) such that \(\{\mb{u}_1, \ldots, \mb{u}_m\}\) is a basis of \(U_1\text{.}\) Then it is an exercise to see that \(\{\mb{v}_1^*, \ldots, \mb{v}_{n - m}^* \}\) is a basis of \(U_1^\circ\text{.}\) This shows the first statement and the second follows from repeating the argument with the dual basis \(\mathcal{B}^*\) and observing that \(\mathcal{B}^{**} = \mathcal{B}\text{.}\)
For the second statement \(\delta \in U_2^\circ\) then for any \(\mb{u} \in U_1 \) we have \(\delta (\mb{u} ) = 0 \) since \(U_1 \leq U_2\text{.}\) Thus \(\delta \in U_1^\circ\text{.}\)
