Suppose \(A\) is a diagonalizable \(n \times n\) real matrix \(A\text{.}\) Then the eigenvalues of \(A\) can be partitioned into the real eigenvalues \(\rho_1, \ldots, \rho_r\) and pairs of complex numbers \(\lambda_1, \bar{\lambda}_1, \ldots, \lambda_t, \bar{\lambda}_t\text{.}\) There is also an eigenbasis
\begin{equation*} \mathcal{B} = \left\{ \mb{v}_1, \ldots, \mb{v}_r , \mb{w}_1, \bar{\mb{w}}_1, \ldots, \mb{w}_t, \bar{\mb{w}}_t \right\} . \end{equation*}
Solutions to the differential equation
\begin{equation*} \dot{\mb{x}} = A \mb{x} \end{equation*}
are real linear combinations of the functions
\begin{equation*} \mb{x}_i (t) = e^{\rho_i t} \mb{v}_i \end{equation*}
for \(1 \leq i \leq r\) and
\begin{align*} \operatorname{Re} \mb{x}_j (t) \amp = \operatorname{Re} \left( e^{\lambda_i t} \mb{w}_i \right), \\ \operatorname{Im} \mb{x}_j (t) \amp = \operatorname{Im} \left( e^{\lambda_i t} \mb{w}_i \right), \end{align*}
